Welcome!

Welcome to this seminar on "Topics in Personnel Psychology". This course is designed to provide you with a comprehensive understanding of the theoretical, methodological, and practical aspects of personnel psychology with a focus on employee selection and performance. This course is not meant to be a comprehensive review of personnel psychology, but rather a in-depth exploration of contemporary topics in personnel psychology. The purpose of this course is to introduce you to the latest research, theory, and methodology in the field, and to help you develop your own research ideas.

The course is structured around three core pillars:

  • Theory: Critically evaluate seminal and contemporary frameworks.
  • Methodology: Analyze research designs and innovations in performance measurement.
  • Research: Evaluate and develop your own research ideas to advance the field of personnel psychology.

By the end of the course, you will develop the skills to design rigorous research, contribute to scholarly discourse, and translate theory into evidence-based practice. I look forward to a engaging and collaborative semester!

Course Objectives

  • Critically evaluate both seminal and contemporary theoretical frameworks in selection and performance research.
  • Analyze methodological challenges and innovative approaches in performance measurement, prediction, and application.
  • Synthesize empirical studies to bridge theoretical insights with practical organizational issues.
  • Learn how to develop a rigorous research proposal that integrates theory, addresses methodological challenges, and demonstrates scholarly contribution.
  • Engage in constructive peer discussion and critique to refine research ideas and foster a collaborative learning environment.

Class Format

  • Weekly 3-hour seminar sessions with brief presentations and peer-discussions of class readings.
  • Instructor-facilitated in-class activities (debates, case studies, article critiques, etc.).
  • Weekly discussion questions: Each student submits 5 thoughtful questions based on the readings by Sunday at Noon.
  • Extended reading list: Each student finds and submits 1 additional recent (~ 5 years old) article weekly. The articles should be published in relevant journal to I/O psychology, or related fields. Your nomination should include a pdf of the article, and a brief (3-4 sentences) description of why you chose this article.
  • Peer review: Each student provides constructive feedback to a peer's research proposal.
  • In-person mid-term exam: An open-ended, in-person essay exam testing knowledge of theoretical and methodological issues personnel psychology
  • Research proposal: Each student develops a research proposal grounded in psychological theory. You will submit a one-pager and a final proposal.

Policy on AI Tools

In this doctoral seminar, we view AI tools as valuable resources to supplement your learning, not replace it. You're welcome to use AI for tasks that enhance your research process, such as organizing your thoughts, refining writing clarity, or summarizing complex texts. However, the work you submit must always reflect your original thought, critical analysis, and independent scholarship. You should also be aware of the limitations of AI and be responsible for any errors or misrepresentations made by AI.

Core Policy: If it's against the rules to ask a human to perform a task designed to assess your understanding, then you shouldn't ask AI to do it either.

Transparency: When you use AI in permitted ways, acknowledge how you utilized the tool.
